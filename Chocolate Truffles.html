Chocolate Truffles

Ingredients

  • 8 oz. (225 g) semisweet chocolate, chopped
  • 1/3 cup (80 ml) heavy cream
  • 1 tbsp. (14 g) unsalted butter
  • Optional: 1 tbsp. liqueur or flavoring (rum, mint extract, etc.)
  • Coatings: cocoa powder, finely chopped nuts, sprinkles, etc.

Instructions

  1. Place the chopped chocolate in a heatproof bowl.
  2. In a small saucepan, heat the heavy cream and butter over medium heat until it starts to simmer (do not bring to a boil).
  3. Pour the hot cream mixture over the chocolate, ensuring it's fully submerged. Let it sit for 2 minutes.
  4. Use a spatula or spoon to stir the mixture until the chocolate is fully melted and the ganache is smooth and glossy.
  5. Optional: Add your chosen liqueur or flavoring and mix well.
  6. Let the ganache cool to room temperature, then cover and refrigerate for about 2 hours or until it's firm enough to scoop and roll.
  7. Use a small cookie scoop or a teaspoon to scoop out a small amount of the ganache, then roll it between your palms to form a small ball, about 1 inch in diameter.
  8. Roll the truffle in your chosen coatings (cocoa powder, nuts, sprinkles, etc.) and place it on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
  9. Repeat with the remaining ganache.
  10. Refrigerate the truffles for at least 30 minutes before serving to allow them to firm up.
